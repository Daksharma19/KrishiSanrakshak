Steps to Get Organic Certification

  1. Step 1: Research & Understand Organic Standards
    Before applying for certification, thoroughly understand your country's organic standards. These guidelines will help you align your practices with the necessary requirements for certification.
  2. Step 2: Select a Certification Body
    Choose an accredited certification agency that is recognized in your region. This will depend on your countryâ€™s specific organic certification program (e.g., USDA Organic in the U.S., EU Organic in Europe).
  3. Step 3: Submit Your Application
    Submit an application with your farming practices, including land management, crop production, pest control methods, and more. Be sure to provide all required documents and information.
  4. Step 4: Farm Inspection
    A certifying inspector will visit your farm to assess your practices and ensure compliance with organic standards. They will review your records, practices, and farm operations.
  5. Step 5: Await Certification Decision
    After the inspection, the certifying body will review your application. If your practices meet the standards, you will be granted organic certification.
  6. Step 6: Annual Inspections & Compliance
    Organic certification is an ongoing process. You must undergo annual inspections to ensure you are maintaining organic practices. You will also need to submit annual reports.
